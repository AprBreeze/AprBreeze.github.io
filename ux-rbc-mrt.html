Mortgage Renewal Tool UX Enhancement

Boosting user confidence with digital mortgage renewal through user-trust building.

Role Interaction Designer
Duration 4 months
Team HomeX
Methods UX Design
Prototype
A/B Testing
Usability Testing

Quick Summary

Problem: High abandonment on the MRT entry page due to a lack of digital trust and a preference for in-branch advice.


Business Goal: Reduce advisor workload by migrating renewals to digital during the summer peak.


Design Intervention: Restructured the one-page landing area into a two-step sequence to focus attention on online renewal benefits.


Result: A research-backed flow that builds user confidence early without adding unnecessary friction or length to the process.

Design Phase 1: Empathize

Business Requirements

Analytics revealed considerable user drop-off on the MRT landing page.
Business insights indicated that clients lacked confidence in the online process and were unconvinced of the digital tool's benefits.


To mitigate this, the business goals were to:

Build Digital Trust: Reinforce credibility early to boost client confidence in online renewals.

Increase Tool Adoption: Increase conversion from the landing page into the actual tool to reduce branch advisor workload during peak season.

Problem Definition

How might we redesign the entry page to clearly communicate the benefits of online renewal and build user trust, without compromising the simple, low-effort experience?

Design Phase 3: Ideate

Solution Proposal

Option 1: The Two-Step Split (Selected)

Separate marketing value from account data into two distinct screens.


Step 1: Full-screen focus on digital benefits, FAQs, and trust indicators.

Step 2: Deep dive into current mortgage details.


UX Rationale: Eliminates visual competition; guarantees users absorb trust-building context before seeing sensitive financial numbers.

Option 2: Single-Page Accordion

Maintain a single-page structure but prioritize layout hierarchy.


Top of Page: Marketing content displayed prominently by default.

Bottom of Page: Mortgage details collapsed into an expandable accordion.


UX Rationale: Keeps the step-count minimal while attempting to push marketing content first, relying on user curiosity to expand details.
