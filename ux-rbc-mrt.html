Mortgage Renewal Tool UX Enhancement

Boosting user confidence with digital mortgage renewal through user-trust building.

Role Interaction Designer
Duration 4 months
Team HomeX
Methods UX Design
Prototype
A/B Testing
Usability Testing

Quick Summary

Problem

High abandonment on the MRT entry page due to a lack of digital trust and a preference for in-branch advice.

Business Goal

Reduce advisor workload by migrating renewals to digital during the summer peak.

Design Intervention

Restructured the one-page landing area into a two-step sequence to focus attention on online renewal benefits.

Result

A research-backed flow that builds user confidence early without adding unnecessary friction or length to the process.

Design Phase 1: Empathize

Business Requirements

Analytics found a considerable user drop-off on the MRT landing page.

Business insights indicated that clients lacked confidence in the online process and were unconvinced of the digital tool's benefits. To mitigate this, the business goals were to:

1. Build Digital Trust:
Reinforce credibility early to boost client confidence in online renewals.

2. Increase Tool Adoption:
Increase conversion from the landing page into the actual tool to reduce branch advisor workload during peak season.

Problem Definition

How might we redesign the entry page to clearly communicate the benefits of online renewal and build user trust, without compromising the simple, low-effort experience?

Design Phase 3: Ideate

Solution Proposal

Option 1: The Two-Step Split

Separate marketing value from account data into two distinct screens.


Step 1: Full-screen focus on digital benefits, FAQs, and trust indicators.

Step 2: Deep dive into current mortgage details.


Rationale: Eliminates visual competition; guarantees users absorb trust-building context before seeing sensitive financial numbers.

Option 2: Single-Page Accordion

Maintain a single-page structure but prioritize layout hierarchy.


Top of Page: Marketing content displayed prominently by default.

Bottom of Page: Mortgage details collapsed into an expandable accordion.


Rationale: Keeps the step-count minimal while attempting to push marketing content first, relying on user curiosity to expand details.

Concerns

Both design options presented clear trade-offs:

Option 1: Two-Step Split

Pros Provided great structure and clear boundaries
Cons Risked making the process feel too long by adding an extra screen

Option 2: Single-Page Accordion

Pros Kept the total steps to a minimum
Cons Risked overloading users with too much information since the marketing content is expanded

Therefore, we set up research to discover which version users preferred...

A/B Testing

I partnered with our team researcher on the study design and conducted the thematic analysis of qualitative user feedback

What users liked about option 1: Readability

"For me it is easier to see information better when it's provided separately on two different items or different information. It's easier for me to read and I do not have the desire to jump because of the amount of text."

"(Split landing page) breaks down the info so it is easier to digest"

"Not too much information in one page feels less overwhelming."

What users liked about option 2: Efficiency

"Version B is expandable which makes it much more convenient to see all of the information on one page. This option is less overwhelming for me."

"More information in one page."

"Found it more compact and easier overall to view at a glance."

However, more cons are observed in option 2

"When the information was combined on one page in Version B, it felt like I was a little overwhelmed and alternating between the two sets of information in a stilted way."

"It wasn't immediately obvious to me that I needed to expand my mortgage selection in the second one."

"i don't like scroll my screen like never ending"
