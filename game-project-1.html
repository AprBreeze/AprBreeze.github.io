Ubisoft NEXT 2024

Level Design Competition Entry - Top 10 in Level Design

Project Overview

During this project, I designed an FPS level incorporating stealth mechanics. The theme of the design competition was Verticality.

In this level, I implemented a multi-layered structure. The player starts from the port, infiltrates the cargo ship to search for the missing agent, explores environmental storytelling, uncovers the secrets hidden on the cargo ship, and ultimately escapes from the top deck.

This Journey Starts From Ideation - Divergence Brainstorming

I applied a divergent mindset to generate a range of relevant ideas, which I documented on my idea board. By identifying connections between related ideas, I was able to develop my ideas to be more comprehensive, refined and creative.

User Research

To better understand user needs, I reviewed two recent critiques of Call of Duty titles and extracted key quotes into engagement codes. These codes act as design insights and directions, ensuring that my work aligns with player expectations and desires.

Some of the Quotes

  • " with communication to team members limited to radio chatter. As a result, there's zero sense of camaraderie"
  • "opting for a thin veil of openness and choice over Call of Duty's once-trademark blockbuster storytelling. It's a shame because you can see a scenario where these missions could've reached the heights of 2019's Lights Out."
  • "no matter how stealthily you approach the situation you're only really delaying the inevitable firefights against seemingly endlessly spawning enemies. And once you are spotted you'll have a near-impossible job trying to lose them."
  • "these open combat missions, they all end up blending into a rinse-and-repeat experience I felt like I was playing over and over, just in different locations."

Engagement Codes

  • Team member/NPC interaction
  • Sense of camaraderie
  • Blockbuster narrative
  • Tactical stealth gameplay
  • Mistake tolerance
  • Avoid Obvious Repetition
  • Various objective type

Mechanics Engagement Breakdown

I created a table to break down the engagement type of each gameplay ingredient that is available in the competition.

This approach allowed me to understand gameplay ingredients from the players' perspective and helped me strategically incorporate these mechanics and craft dynamic gameplay experiences by thoughtfully using different engagement types throughout my level.

Level Beat Pacing Sheet

I developed a level pacing sheet to strategically map out the pace and narrative flow of the level. Each beat is assigned a specific engagement type, paired with corresponding events and gameplay activities. I planned the environment to create a dynamic gameplay experience, ensuring that various gameplay and environmental shifts interweave dynamically. This approach allows for a well-balanced, engaging level structure that keeps players continuously immersed.

Feedback from Ubisoft Judges

Pros:

  • 1. The overall layout is well-structured.
  • 2. The mission objective is clear and easy to follow.
  • 3. Effective use of notes and ally NPCs enhances the experience.
  • 4. Key and room placements are well thought out.
  • 5. Ammo distribution is balanced and sufficient.

Cons:

  • 1. Some areas of the level feel claustrophobic due to tight walkways.
  • 2. Certain placement issues resulted in gaps in the level, allowing the AI to detect the player unexpectedly.
  • 3. These gaps also caused AI pathfinding issues, preventing them from reaching the player in some cases.
  • 4. The ending feels rushed, as the ladder leading to the next section is placed just outside the room.

Lessons I learned and will put in future practice:

  • 1. I need to develop better spatial management skills to avoid overly narrow areas.
  • 2. It's also crucial to align modular level elements more accurately to ensure proper AI functionality.
  • 3. While I aimed for players to immediately understand their choices at the end, particularly favoring the rescue option, this led me to focus disproportionately on refining that specific experience. I neglected the other option, which taught me a vital lesson: in future projects, I must consider all player choices and ensure each provides meaningful gameplay.
