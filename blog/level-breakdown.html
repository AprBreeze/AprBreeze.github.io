Learn from Systematically Breaking Down Levels

Today, after a lot of research and organizing information online, I realized there isn’t a convincing structure for systematically breaking down game levels. Some say breakdowns should involve listing all elements and identifying their design purposes. However, I feel this approach lacks guidance for level design because level design is a complex craft where different dimensions vary greatly yet are interconnected.

However, I feel this approach lacks guidance for level design because level design is a complex craft where different dimensions vary greatly yet are interconnected. If I break down everything to the smallest units like a system designer, I lose the high-level/cohesive perspective that a level designer needs.

That said, I've been facing repeated setbacks in interviews, and I have to admit that my ability to deconstruct existing products is objectively lacking, something interviewers care about a lot.

A New Framework for Level Analysis

So today, I tried combining online insights with my own level design workflow and reflections, and I created a structure for level breakdowns that feels right to me.

I divided level design into four sections. Although these areas overlap, separating them helps keep the analysis clear:

1. Mechanics

  • Gameplay type - Core gameplay mechanics and their variations
  • Experience goals - What the level aims to achieve
  • Design constraints - Technical and gameplay limitations

2. Narrative

  • Narrative pacing - How the story unfolds through the level
  • Spatial storytelling - How the macro-level scene content tells the story

3. Space

  • Path guidance - How players are directed through the level
  • Spatial storytelling - How micro-level content layout supports the narrative

4. Pacing

  • Experience flow - The rhythm and flow of gameplay moments
  • Experience intensity - The peaks and valleys of player engagement

This analysis structure matches my actual level design workflow, but the order is slightly different.

When designing levels, I usually prioritize pacing first — defining the level's rhythm (including narrative pacing) — before working on space (2D layouts and 3D blockouts).

However, for breakdowns, I adjusted the order: since narrative and space are more objective and visible, analyzing them first makes the subsequent pacing analysis more accurate and grounded.

Moving Forward

Moving forward, I plan to use this framework to break down levels and seek feedback from industry veterans to validate my approach. This structured method should help me better understand existing games and improve my ability to discuss level design in professional contexts.
