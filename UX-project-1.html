Problem Discovery

Our team's shared love for pets inspired our brainstorming sessions to identify key topics and pain points we wanted to address. We drew inspiration from trending products like Pet Talk and pet communication buttons, which explore advanced interactions between pets and their owners. These tools sparked our curiosity about fostering deeper bonds and enhancing understanding through technology.

Key Research Questions

Behavior Interpretation:

What challenges do pet owners face when interpreting their pets' behaviors, and how do these challenges affect their ability to meet their pets' needs?

Technology Usability:

What are pet owners' experiences with the usability and effectiveness of current pet-related technologies, and what limitations do they encounter?

New Tool Expectations:

What are pet owners' main concerns and expectations regarding new tools designed to improve pet-owner communication?

Problem Definition

Key Area of Focus

Interpreting Pet Behavior:

Identify challenges in understanding pets' emotions, common misinterpretations, and frustrations.

Experience with Pet Technologies:

Explore usability issues, data reliability, and communication limitations in current tools.

Improving Pet Communication:

Uncover desired features and expectations for technology to enhance pet-owner bonds.

Pet owners struggle to understand their pets' behaviors and needs, leading to poor pet care and frustration.

Research Insights

Tracking & Monitoring

Pet owners use various tools to track pet activities and monitor their well-being, from simple notebooks to cameras.

Health Concerns

Sudden health issues are difficult to detect early without expensive tests, causing frustration and worry.

Information Sources

Owners rely on online communities and forums for advice, but are skeptical about the accuracy of pet communication apps.

Affinity Diagram

We created an affinity diagram to cluster similar codes into themes, enabling us to identify common problems and concerns faced by our potential users. This process allowed us to extract analytical value from the interview scripts, transforming raw textual content into actionable design insights. By visually organizing the data, the affinity diagram also helped us uncover deeper patterns and relationships that informed our design decisions.

Research Report

After coding all the scripts, we consolidated each team member's analyses to produce a comprehensive research report. From the data, we identified seven major themes that emerged from the coding process.

Theme 1:

People want pet-related content and products that extend ways to engage with their pets.

7/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 2:

People worry that the application failed to provide reliable information.

8/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 3:

Participants concerned that pet types and their individual differences make the app unusable.

5/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 4:

Participants feel skeptical about both pet behaviors and the usability of current solutions.

8/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 5:

Current solutions provide limited alternatives and do not fully address users' problems.

7/12 participants reported similar experiences.

Theme 6:

Users prefer solutions that help humans understand animals, rather than expecting animals to understand humans.

12/12 participants expressed this preference.

Theme 7:

Many of our interview participants used or expressed their interest in pet-related products. Those people tend to be willing to pay for these products if the price is reasonable.

7/12 participants reported interest while 4/7 are willing to pay a reasonable price.

Usability Testing & Iteration

Methodology and Results

We conducted five in-person usability testing sessions: one with an expert and four with classmates. The goal was to assess whether users could comprehend the application's structure and complete the assigned tasks successfully.


From careful observation and user feedback, we identified the following key insights:

  • Complex Application Structure
    Users found the overall structure of the application overly complicated and difficult to navigate.
  • Generative AI Learning Curve
    The integration of generative AI technology posed a significant challenge, particularly for users unfamiliar with tools like ChatGPT.
  • AI Model Selection Confusion
    The AI model selection feature was especially difficult for users to understand, creating confusion during the testing process.
  • Overwhelming Interaction Buttons
    The interface had too many interaction buttons, leaving users unsure about their functionality. This was a recurring point of feedback during the sessions.

Iteration Process

Using feedback from usability testing, we refined the wireframes to create interfaces that are more intuitive and user-friendly. The major changes included:


  • Simplified Features
    Consolidated functionality into a single AI model focused solely on pet behaviour interpretation.
  • Highlighted Core Functions
    Featured frequently used functions on the home page to clearly communicate the app's primary purpose.
  • Removed Secondary Features
    Removed secondary features, such as "upload" and "take pictures," to avoid distractions from the app's main goal.
  • Restructured Navigation
    Redesigned the navigation flow to align with intuitive usage logic: See the pet → Pet interpretation → Further analysis → Share.
  • Reduced Interaction Buttons
    Combined and reorganized interactive buttons across pages to reduce users' mental load.
  • Enhanced Icon Clarity
    Improved icon design to minimize visual ambiguity and ensure clear communication of their functions.

Reflection & Conclusion

Challenges Overcomed

  • Cognitive Overload
    Initially, our app aimed to implement too many features, resulting in a cluttered and confusing user flow. Feedback from industry experts helped us identify the need to streamline the app by removing unnecessary features. This experience taught us that simplicity is key—delivering the right solutions with minimal cognitive load creates the best user experience.
  • Finding Innovative Approach
    Designing for a relatively new and innovative concept meant there were few mature examples to draw inspiration from. To address this, we abstracted and restructured our app flows into simple, user-centred concepts like "see pets, know pets, share pets". Usability testing further guided us in aligning the app's functionality with user expectations, helping us refine the flow.
  • Developing Graphic Design Skills
    Graphic design has not been my strongest skill, but collaboration with peers significantly improved my abilities. Additionally, I leveraged generative AI tools to accelerate my learning and productivity. Now I am more capable of leveraging user experience by having readable and pleasing colors, composition, shapes and icons.

Overall Reflection

  • From Start to End Design Practice
    Gained hands-on experience in the complete interface design process for a mobile app, from initial ideas to the final high-fidelity prototype.
  • The Value of User Research
    Learned that understanding user needs is the essential first step in the design process and the foundation for creating meaningful and effective solutions.
  • Effective Idea Generation
    Throughout the design process, we conducted multiple brainstorming sessions using the Crazy 8s method to generate and refine ideas. I found this practice invaluable as it encouraged unrestricted creativity while channelling it into useful solutions.
  • The Importance of Teamwork
    Recognized the crucial role of collaboration in design. At times, our team's vision diverged, highlighting the need for effective discussion facilitation to align goals and ensure cohesion.
  • Practiced Graphic Design Skills
    Improved my graphic design abilities by learning from peers during the creation of the high-fidelity prototype.
