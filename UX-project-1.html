Problem Discovery

Our team's shared love for pets inspired our brainstorming sessions to identify key topics and pain points we wanted to address. We drew inspiration from trending products like Pet Talk and pet communication buttons, which explore advanced interactions between pets and their owners. These tools sparked our curiosity about fostering deeper bonds and enhancing understanding through technology.

Key Research Questions

Behavior Interpretation:

What challenges do pet owners face when interpreting their pets' behaviors, and how do these challenges affect their ability to meet their pets' needs?

Technology Usability:

What are pet owners' experiences with the usability and effectiveness of current pet-related technologies, and what limitations do they encounter?

New Tool Expectations:

What are pet owners' main concerns and expectations regarding new tools designed to improve pet-owner communication?

Problem Definition

Key Area of Focus

Interpreting Pet Behavior:

Identify challenges in understanding pets' emotions, common misinterpretations, and frustrations.

Experience with Pet Technologies:

Explore usability issues, data reliability, and communication limitations in current tools.

Improving Pet Communication:

Uncover desired features and expectations for technology to enhance pet-owner bonds.

Problem Statement

Pet owners struggle to understand their pets' behaviors and needs, leading to poor pet care and frustration.

User Research

We conducted 12 interviews with a diverse group of potential users and stakeholders, including:

  • Pet owners (Primary Audience)
  • Pet Industry Professionals

Key Findings from User Interviews

"I usually spend about 2 to 3 hours with her after school every day. I also use tools like notebooks or other methods to track her to-do list and ensure nothing is left unfinished. Additionally, we use a camera to check on our pet and make sure everything is fine."

"My dog had brain cancer, and it happened very suddenly. It's difficult to understand without resorting to expensive diagnostic tests."

"Sometimes I try to understand her behaviour by searching on the internet or using forums. I rely on groups or chats with experienced pet owners where I can ask questions online. I haven't downloaded many pet communication apps because I'm not sure how accurate they are. On the rare occasions I do use such apps, it's mostly just for fun."

Research Insights

Tracking & Monitoring

Pet owners use various tools to track pet activities and monitor their well-being, from simple notebooks to cameras.

Health Concerns

Sudden health issues are difficult to detect early without expensive tests, causing frustration and worry.

Information Sources

Owners rely on online communities and forums for advice, but are skeptical about the accuracy of pet communication apps.
