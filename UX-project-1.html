Design Process Overview

Problem Discovery

Starting with AI technology and our team's interest in pets, I defined our research path. We first explored the feasibility of computer vision and natural language models, then analyzed observable pet behaviors from academic research. Subsequent market research helped us identify existing solutions and competitors. This iterative process led to actionable insights and clarified our core research questions:

How do users currently observe and interpret pet behavior?

What are user perceptions regarding AI integration into pet behavior interpretation?

Research Insights

Tracking & Monitoring

Pet owners use various tools to track pet activities and monitor their well-being, from simple notebooks to cameras.

Health Concerns

Sudden health issues are difficult to detect early without expensive tests, causing frustration and worry.

Information Sources

Owners rely on online communities and forums for advice, but are skeptical about the accuracy of pet communication apps.

Affinity Diagram

We created an affinity diagram to cluster similar codes into themes, enabling us to identify common problems and concerns faced by our potential users.

This process allowed us to extract analytical value from the interview scripts, transforming raw textual content into actionable design insights. By visually organizing the data, the affinity diagram also helped us uncover deeper patterns and relationships that informed our design decisions.

Research Report

After coding all the scripts, we consolidated each team member's analyses to produce a comprehensive research report. From the data, we identified seven major themes that emerged from the coding process.

Theme 1:

People want pet-related content and products that extend ways to engage with their pets.

7/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 2:

People worry that the application failed to provide reliable information.

8/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 3:

Participants concerned that pet types and their individual differences make the app unusable.

5/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 4:

Participants feel skeptical about both pet behaviors and the usability of current solutions.

8/12 interview participants reported similar ideas.

Theme 5:

Current solutions provide limited alternatives and do not fully address users' problems.

7/12 participants reported similar experiences.

Theme 6:

Users prefer solutions that help humans understand animals, rather than expecting animals to understand humans.

12/12 participants expressed this preference.

Theme 7:

Many of our interview participants used or expressed their interest in pet-related products. Those people tend to be willing to pay for these products if the price is reasonable.

7/12 participants reported interest while 4/7 are willing to pay a reasonable price.

Usability Testing & Iteration

Methodology and Results

Usability Testing Approach

We conducted five in-person usability testing sessions: one with an expert and four with classmates. The goal was to assess whether users could comprehend the application's structure and complete the assigned tasks successfully.

5 Testing Sessions
1 Expert Review
4 User Tests

Key Insights from Testing

Complex Application Structure

Users found the overall structure of the application overly complicated and difficult to navigate.

Generative AI Learning Curve

The integration of generative AI technology posed a significant challenge, particularly for users unfamiliar with tools like ChatGPT.

AI Model Selection Confusion

The AI model selection feature was especially difficult for users to understand, creating confusion during the testing process.

Overwhelming Interaction Buttons

The interface had too many interaction buttons, leaving users unsure about their functionality. This was a recurring point of feedback during the sessions.

Iteration

Using feedback from usability testing, we refined the wireframes to create interfaces that are more intuitive and user-friendly. The major changes included:

  • Simplified Features
    Consolidated functionality into a single AI model focused solely on pet behaviour interpretation.
  • Highlighted Core Functions
    Featured frequently used functions on the home page to clearly communicate the app's primary purpose.
  • Removed Secondary Features
    Removed secondary features, such as "upload" and "take pictures," to avoid distractions from the app's main goal.
  • Restructured Navigation
    Redesigned the navigation flow to align with intuitive usage logic: See the pet → Pet interpretation → Further analysis → Share.
  • Reduced Interaction Buttons
    Combined and reorganized interactive buttons across pages to reduce users' mental load.
  • Enhanced Icon Clarity
    Improved icon design to minimize visual ambiguity and ensure clear communication of their functions.

Reflection & Conclusion

  • Cognitive Load Management
    Wanting too much often leads to a cluttered interface, high cognitive load and a poor user experience.
  • Developing Graphic Design Skills
    Collaborative work and leveraging AI tools significantly enhanced my graphic design skills, improving readability, setting a consistent style system, and enhancing visual presentation.
  • User Testing is Crucial to Break Biased Assumptions
    We assumed that users are firmiliar with AI models and features, but in fact, most of them are lost in our initial testing. User testing breaked our biased asumptions and led us to a more intuitive design.
  • The Value of User Research
    Reaffirmed that research is such a powerful way to empathize with users and understand their needs.
  • Effective Idea Generation
    Repeated Crazy 8s brainstorming sessions proved invaluable for generating diverse, creative, yet actionable solutions.
  • Crucial Team Collaboration
    Highlighted the importance of effective discussion and alignment to maintain a cohesive vision, even when perspectives diverged.
  • End to End Design Process
    Gained hands-on experience across the entire mobile app design process, from ideation to high-fidelity prototyping.
