Voting Accessibility Design

Make visible accessibility come true.

Role Service Designer, UX Researcher
Duration 2 weeks
Team 5 Designers
Methods Service Mapping Exploratory Research Storyboarding

Quick Summary

We redesigned the user interaction flow of Elections Ontario's digital application to provide a smoother and more accessible voting experience. The updated flow guides users step-by-step and introduces accommodation prompts at key moments to ensure timely support is available. Beyond the app, we also identified and proposed changes to related service touchpoints to support the successful implementation of the improved end-to-end service journey.

We conducted a 1 hour client interview to understand the problem that Elections Ontario (EO) is facing. EO aims to ensure every eligible citizen can cast their ballot. However, they have encountered growth barrier.

Further more, the user insight data provided by Elections Ontario showed a significant growth potential for accessibility use, especially if we compare to the most recent disability data.

User Problem

After we identified the potential growth opportunities, we conducted exploratory research to validate if the problem is a real challenge that our users are facing.

Role Play:

To uncover unmet needs in the voting journey, we designed a generative research activity rooted in empathy. We created a deck of identity cards representing a range of voters (e.g. an elderly person with arthritis, someone with limited vision, or a voter with hearing loss) and paired them with scenario cards that captured common voting tasks like applying for a special ballot or locating an accessible polling station. Each participant was asked to draw one identity and one scenario at random. From there, we facilitated a guided role-play interview, encouraging them to think through the experience from that identity's perspective.

Our questions focused on three core themes:

  • Clarity ‚Äì Which parts of the process were confusing or hard to follow for this voter?
  • Expectations ‚Äì What would they reasonably expect to happen at each step?
  • Opportunities ‚Äì Where could the experience be improved to better support this voter?
Role Play Benefits/Advantage:

Effective in revealing edge cases: helped us explore intersections of needs, like how low vision and low tech confidence compound barriers. Such complexity is difficult to capture in standard interviews unless recruiting very large and specific samples.

Why Not Interview Rationale:
  • Limited time and budget to conduct user interviews
  • We intentionally chose role play over direct user interviews because we were designing for a diverse population with a range of accessibility needs, many of whom we couldn't easily reach.

Design Phase 3: Ideate

Storyboard Testing

We developed two storyboards. The first mapped our ideal user journey and, through co-design sessions with users, helped uncover gaps that designers might overlook.

From this, we proposed improvements (marked with yellow notes) to enhance the user flow.

The second storyboard reflects the final, improved journey.

Insights from storyboard testing:

  • Accessibility features received positive feedback and were seen as effective.
  • Trust emerged as a critical theme ‚Äî users wanted better transparency and progress tracking.

    • Note: Due to time constraints, we prioritized implementing accessibility features. However, storyboard testing revealed that trust-related features, such as tracking and notifications, are just as essential for user confidence and clarity.

    Solution Proposal

    A design overhaul of the Elections Ontario app's accessibility experience, delivering transparent service information, enabling confident accommodation bookings via a streamlined appointment system, and boosting trust through an integrated review feature.

    Design Phase 4: Prototype

    Low-Fidelity Wireframes

    We created low-fidelity wireframes to test different layout and interaction options:

  • Survey flow: Compared long-scroll vs. step-by-step formats for clarity and ease of use.
  • Add representative: Tested early access throughout the process vs. limiting it to a single page to balance user autonomy and permissions.
  • View reviews: Explored separate links per voting method vs. a single universal link for consistency.

    • High-Fidelity Prototype

    For the high-fidelity prototype, we refined the visuals and ensured the design met usability and accessibility standards through clear hierarchy, contrast, and intuitive navigation.

    Implementation Plan

    Cost-Effective Solution

    Conservatively estimate the app dev cost at 300k. Also considering the government organization requires a longer regulatory compliance process, system stability and security, we expect the costs of the dev team to be 50% more than regular app development costs.


    Therefore, the final cost estimate is 400k to 450k. Which client originally planned to have 1 million to do an entire app overhaul, our solution proposed a solution that added to the existing application, which significantly reduced costs while delivering the core outcomes.

    Strategic Approach

    Our approach focuses on enhancing the existing application rather than building from scratch, delivering maximum value while minimizing disruption to current users and systems.

    Impact

    Client Satisfaction

    The client expressed strong satisfaction with the outcomes of our consulting project. They believe our solution can significantly improve internal resource efficiency, especially in managing accommodation equipment and coordinating the support team.

    Problem Reframing

    We redefined the original challenge from simply "boosting app adoption" to a more meaningful question:

    How might we build user trust, understanding, and confidence in using accessibility voting options?

    This shift transformed the app from a basic navigation tool into a bridge that connects users with essential information and empowers them to participate confidently in the voting process.

    Outcome-Driven Design

    Beyond proposing features, we also defined success metrics such as increased voter confidence and usage of accessibility options. We included a detailed implementation plan to help Elections Ontario evaluate service performance and ensure feasibility.

    Cross-Department Alignment

    Through service mapping and co-design workshops, we facilitated alignment among internal teams across departments. This helped unify goals and deepen understanding of user needs‚Äîespecially for senior voters and those with disabilities.

    Sharpened Design Focus

    Our research and recommendations helped the EO team clearly identify critical user pain points and prioritize features accordingly, strengthening the clarity and impact of design decisions.

    Reflection

    Scope of Accessibility Coverage

    Our work primarily focused on voters with hearing and mobility needs. A future direction is to better support users with visual or cognitive impairments, ensuring our solutions are truly inclusive for all accessibility needs.

    Beyond Digital Solutions

    While we addressed the special ballot process, we recognized the need to also improve accessibility support for voters at physical polling stations. This represents an opportunity to create a more holistic voting experience.

    Research-Driven Decisions

    A data-driven approach grounded our decisions in real user feedback, ensuring relevance and impact. This methodology proved essential for building credible, user-centered solutions.

    Cross-Functional Collaboration

    Collaborating across client teams‚ÄîUX, PMs, IT, and the Digital Solutions Director‚Äîhelped us align goals and build practical, scalable solutions. This collaborative approach was crucial for ensuring feasibility and stakeholder buy-in.
